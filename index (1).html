Business requirements document (BRD)

Project overview

Redesign of the patient intake and service coordination workflow at Integral Care to reduce time-to-service, eliminate manual data entry errors, and enable coordinators to manage a larger account portfolio through digital automation and standardized processes.

Business problem

The current intake process averages 7–10 days due to manual paper forms, phone-only access, and no automated routing. Coordinators spend an estimated 35% of weekly capacity on data entry tasks rather than client-facing work. Error rates in EHR records are estimated at 22%, leading to duplicate outreach and delayed care plans.

Stakeholders

  • Service coordinators — primary users of intake and EHR workflows
  • Clinical team — downstream consumers of intake data for care planning
  • IT department — responsible for EHR configuration and portal build
  • Operations leadership — accountable for capacity and retention KPIs
  • Clients / patients — end users of the intake experience

Functional requirements

FR-01System shall provide a digital intake form accessible via web portal and mobile, with field validation and required field enforcement.P1
FR-02Completed intake forms shall automatically populate corresponding fields in the EHR (My Avatar NX) without manual re-entry.P1
FR-03System shall auto-route new intakes to the appropriate service coordinator based on availability and caseload rules.P1
FR-04Coordinators shall have access to a real-time triage queue with priority scoring based on urgency flags and wait time.P1
FR-05System shall provide standardized success plan templates with pre-populated fields derived from intake data.P2
FR-06Clients shall receive automated status updates at key workflow milestones (intake received, coordinator assigned, plan created).P2
FR-07A Power BI dashboard shall display real-time KPIs including intake volume, resolution time, retention rate, and plan completion rate.P3

Constraints

  • Must comply with HIPAA data privacy requirements
  • EHR (My Avatar NX) integration must not disrupt existing clinical workflows
  • Implementation timeline: 90 days for Phase 1 (FR-01 through FR-04)
