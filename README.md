## Overview

A full end-to-end business analyst case study built around a real healthcare service coordination workflow. This project demonstrates core BA competencies through a realistic scenario grounded in hands-on experience managing 50+ client accounts at Integral Care (Austin, TX).

The scenario: analyzing and optimizing a broken patient intake process that was averaging 7–10 days, driven by paper forms, manual EHR entry, and no automated triage routing.

---

## What's Inside

| Tab | Deliverable | Description |
|-----|-------------|-------------|
| **Process map** | As-Is / To-Be workflow | Side-by-side comparison of current vs optimized intake flow with bottlenecks, delays, and improvements called out |
| **Gap analysis** | Gap breakdown table | 7 process areas assessed by severity (Critical / High / Medium) with current state, target state, and accountable owner |
| **KPI dashboard** | Live charts | Monthly intake volume, average resolution time trend, and retention rate improvement tracked over 6 months |
| **Requirements doc** | Business Requirements Document (BRD) | Full BRD with problem statement, stakeholder map, 7 functional requirements (FR-01 through FR-07) with priority levels, and HIPAA constraints |
| **ROI model** | Financial impact analysis | Cost/benefit breakdown showing $87K annual savings, $42K implementation cost, 5.8-month payback, and 521% 3-year ROI |

---

## Key Findings

- **78% reduction** in average intake time (7–10 days → 1–2 days) through digital form automation and EHR auto-sync
- **65% reduction** in data entry errors by eliminating manual transcription
- **40% increase** in coordinator capacity freed from administrative tasks
- **521% ROI** over 3 years with a payback period of ~5.8 months
